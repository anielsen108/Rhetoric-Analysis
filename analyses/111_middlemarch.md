**Evidence:** "thrown into relief by poor dress"
**Effect:** Reveals Dorothea's unconscious asceticism.
**Near-miss:** Oxymoron is paradox compressed to a two-word collision ('living death'); antithesis balances opposites without asserting both are true.

### **Allusion** (uh-LOO-zhun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Reference to other work.
**Evidence:** "Italian painters"
**Effect:** Elevates Dorothea to religious/artistic ideal.
**Near-miss:** Quotation announces its borrowing; allusion smuggles it. Metalepsis is allusion at a further remove, referencing through a chain.

### **Simile** (SIM-ih-lee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Explicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "those in which the Blessed Virgin"
**Effect:** Sanctifies Dorothea's plainness.
**Near-miss:** Drop the 'like' and it becomes metaphor. An epic simile extends the comparison over many lines with its own internal scene.
