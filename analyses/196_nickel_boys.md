**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Meaning opposite to surface.
**Evidence:** "trouble"
**Effect:** Indicts institutional violence through bitter understatement.
**Near-miss:** Sarcasm is verbal irony weaponized at a target. Paradox asserts a real contradiction; irony merely means other than it says.

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "stubborn"
**Effect:** Makes vegetation into memorial resistance.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

### **Euphemism** (YOO-fuh-miz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mild expression for harsh reality.
**Evidence:** "secret graveyard"
**Effect:** Uses institutional language for atrocity.
**Near-miss:** Periphrasis is roundabout wording for any reason; euphemism specifically launders the unpleasant.
