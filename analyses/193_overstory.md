## PASSAGE

[L1] The best arguments in the world won't change a person's mind. The only thing that can do that is a good story.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Hyperbole** (hy-PER-buh-lee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Exaggeration.
**Evidence:** "The best arguments in the world"
**Effect:** Maximizes claim about arguments' failure.
**Near-miss:** Its opposite pole is understatement/litotes. When exaggeration collapses into the trivial for comic effect, that's bathos.

### **Litotes** (LY-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Understatement.
**Evidence:** "The only thing"
**Effect:** Makes exclusive claim through restriction.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Antithesis** (an-TIH-thuh-sis)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Balanced opposition.
**Evidence:** "won't change"
**Effect:** Sets rational persuasion against narrative.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
