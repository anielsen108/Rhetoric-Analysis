# Passage 033: "Do Not Go Gentle into That Good Night"

**Dylan Thomas** · *Botteghe Oscure* · 1951 | Selected passage

**Occasion/Pressure:** This lyric passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A third-person or impersonal voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through imperative mood, metaphoric compression, and passionate repetition, Thomas creates syntax of defiance. The form enacts resistance to death—the commands pile up, the metaphors accumulate, the repetitions insist. This is syntax as battle cry.

---

## PASSAGE

[L1] Do not go gentle into that good night,
[L2] Old age should burn and rave at close of day;
[L3] Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Imperative** (im-PAIR-uh-tiv)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Command form of verb.
**Evidence:** "Rage, rage"
**Effect:** Creates urgency and emotional intensity; directly addresses the dying.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “Rage, rage” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Euphemism** (YOO-feh-miz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mild expression for harsh reality.
**Evidence:** "that good night"
**Effect:** Softens death linguistically while rage against it intensifies emotionally.
**Near-miss:** Periphrasis is roundabout wording for any reason; euphemism specifically launders the unpleasant.

### **Personification** (per-sah-nif-ih-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L2
**Definition:** Giving human qualities to non-human things.
**Evidence:** "close"
**Effect:** Makes natural processes seem like conscious agents.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "Do not go gentle into that good night"
**Effect:** Transforms abstract death into concrete images of darkness and light.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Epizeuxis** (ep-ih-ZOOK-sis)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Immediate repetition of word for emphasis.
**Evidence:** "Rage, rage"
**Effect:** Intensifies emotion; creates drumbeat of defiance.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
