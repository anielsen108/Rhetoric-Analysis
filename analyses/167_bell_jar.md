## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Simile** (SIM-uh-lee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Explicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "the way the eye of a tornado must feel"
**Effect:** Makes depression into meteorological phenomenon.
**Near-miss:** Drop the 'like' and it becomes metaphor. An epic simile extends the comparison over many lines with its own internal scene.

### **Personification** (per-sah-nuh-fuh-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Human qualities to abstractions.
**Evidence:** "eye of a tornado"
**Effect:** Makes natural phenomenon sentient.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Oxymoron** (ahk-see-MOR-ahn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Contradictory terms.
**Evidence:** "still"
**Effect:** Captures paradox of depressive calm amid chaos.
**Near-miss:** Paradox is a contradictory *statement* that unfolds; oxymoron is the two-word spark.
