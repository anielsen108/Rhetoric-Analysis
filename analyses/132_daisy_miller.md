**Definition:** Meaning opposite to surface.
**Evidence:** "grateful for having found the formula"
**Effect:** Exposes Winterbourne's need to categorize rather than understand.
**Near-miss:** Sarcasm is verbal irony weaponized at a target. Paradox asserts a real contradiction; irony merely means other than it says.

### **Apposition** (ap-uh-ZIH-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Noun phrase restating another.
**Evidence:** "a flirt—a pretty American flirt"
**Effect:** Refines categorization with qualification.
**Near-miss:** Parenthesis interrupts without renaming; apposition always re-identifies the neighboring noun.

### **Euphemism** (YOO-fuh-miz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mild expression for blunt reality.
**Evidence:** "relations with young ladies"
**Effect:** Shows Winterbourne's prissy formality.
**Near-miss:** Periphrasis is roundabout wording for any reason; euphemism specifically launders the unpleasant.
