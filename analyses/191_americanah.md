## PASSAGE

[L1] Princeton, in the summer, smelled of nothing, and although Ifemelu liked the tranquil greenness of the many trees, the clean streets and stately homes, the delicately overpriced shops, and the quiet, abiding air of earned grace, it was this, the lack of a smell, that most appealed to her.

**Evidence:** "lack of"
**Effect:** Shows immigrant's relationship to privilege as defined by absence.
**Near-miss:** Oxymoron is paradox compressed to a two-word collision ('living death'); antithesis balances opposites without asserting both are true.

### **Oxymoron** (ahk-see-MOR-ahn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Contradictory terms.
**Evidence:** "delicately overpriced"
**Effect:** Mocks pretension of expensive boutiques.
**Near-miss:** Paradox is a contradictory *statement* that unfolds; oxymoron is the two-word spark.
