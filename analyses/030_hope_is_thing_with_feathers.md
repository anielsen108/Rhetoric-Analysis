## PASSAGE

[L1] "Hope" is the thing with feathers -
[L2] That perches in the soul -
[L3] And sings the tune without the words -
[L4] And never stops - at all -

**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Sustained comparison throughout passage.
**Evidence:** "“Hope“ is the thing with feathers -"
**Effect:** Makes abstract emotion concrete, visual, and tangible.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “"Hope" is the thing with feathers -” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Personification** (per-sah-nif-ih-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L2
**Definition:** Attribution of human qualities to non-human things.
**Evidence:** "perches"
**Effect:** Makes hope active, living, present.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Paradox** (PAIR-uh-doks)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Seemingly contradictory statement.
**Evidence:** "tune without the words"
**Effect:** Suggests hope is felt rather than articulated; it transcends language.
**Near-miss:** Oxymoron is paradox compressed to a two-word collision ('living death'); antithesis balances opposites without asserting both are true.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Asyndeton** (uh-SIN-duh-ton)
**Lines:** L3–4
**Definition:** Omission of conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "And"
**Effect:** Creates staccato rhythm; each quality of hope stands separately.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
