## PASSAGE

[L1] Arthur Less is a failed novelist about to turn fifty. A wedding invitation arrives in the mail: his boyfriend of the past nine years is engaged to someone else.

**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Meaning opposite to surface.
**Evidence:** "is engaged to someone else"
**Effect:** Makes "boyfriend" status immediately obsolete.
**Near-miss:** Sarcasm is verbal irony weaponized at a target. Paradox asserts a real contradiction; irony merely means other than it says.

### **Litotes** (LY-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Understatement.
**Evidence:** "failed novelist"
**Effect:** Opens with protagonist's low self-image.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.

### **Juxtaposition** (juhk-stuh-puh-ZI-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Placing contrasting elements together.
**Evidence:** "fifty. A wedding"
**Effect:** Doubles protagonist's crisis.
**Near-miss:** Antithesis states its contrast in balanced grammar; juxtaposition just sets the two things down and walks away.
