# Passage 074: Faust Opening

**Johann Wolfgang von Goethe** · *Faust (translated by Walter Kaufmann)* · 1808 | Part I

**Occasion/Pressure:** This lyric passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A first-person voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through perfect tense connecting past to present despair, accumulating catalog, and climactic "worst of all," Goethe creates syntax of intellectual disillusion. Form IS the failed quest—grammar that lists achievements only to frame them as failures.

## PASSAGE

[L2] jurisprudence and medicine, too,
[L3] and, worst of all, theology
[L4] from end to end with diligence.
