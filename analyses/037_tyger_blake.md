## PASSAGE

[L1] Tyger Tyger, burning bright,
[L2] In the forests of the night;
[L3] What immortal hand or eye,
[L4] Could frame thy fearful symmetry?

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Apostrophe** (uh-PAH-struh-fee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Direct address to absent entity.
**Evidence:** "Tyger Tyger"
**Effect:** Creates intimacy and intensity; makes philosophical question personal.
**Near-miss:** Direct address speaks to the present audience ('Reader, I married him' sits between the two); invocation is apostrophe petitioning for aid.

### **Rhetorical Question** (rih-TOR-ih-kul KWES-chun)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Question asked for effect, not answer.
**Evidence:** "What immortal hand or eye"
**Effect:** Expresses wonder and terror; implies no answer exists.
**Near-miss:** Hypophora asks and then *answers its own* question; aporia performs genuine (or staged) doubt.

### **Oxymoron** (ok-see-MOR-on)
**Lines:** L4
**Definition:** Combination of contradictory terms.
**Evidence:** "fearful symmetry"
**Effect:** Captures the paradox of creation: order and terror unified.
**Near-miss:** Paradox is a contradictory *statement* that unfolds; oxymoron is the two-word spark.

### **Synesthesia** (sin-es-THEE-zhuh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mixing of sensory impressions.
**Evidence:** "burning bright"
**Effect:** Makes the tiger's appearance intense, almost supernatural.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “burning bright” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.
