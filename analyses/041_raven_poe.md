## PASSAGE

[L1] Once upon a midnight dreary, while I pondered, weak and weary,
[L2] Over many a quaint and curious volume of forgotten lore—
[L3] While I nodded, nearly napping, suddenly there came a tapping,
[L4] As of some one gently rapping, rapping at my chamber door.

**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “napping, suddenly there came a tapping” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Archaism** (AR-kay-iz-um)
**Lines:** L2
**Definition:** Use of old-fashioned language.
**Evidence:** "quaint and curious volume of forgotten lore"
**Effect:** Creates Gothic atmosphere; suggests speaker lives in past.
**Near-miss:** Allusion points to a specific prior text; archaism borrows a period's diction wholesale without citing anyone.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Alliteration** (uh-lit-uh-RAY-shun)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Repetition of initial consonant sounds.
**Evidence:** "nodded, nearly napping"
**Effect:** Creates musical, hypnotic quality; emphasizes key words.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.

### **Epizeuxis** (ep-ih-ZOOK-sis)
**Lines:** L4
**Definition:** Immediate repetition for emphasis.
**Evidence:** "rapping, rapping"
**Effect:** Creates obsessive focus on the sound; builds tension.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
