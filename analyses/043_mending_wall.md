## PASSAGE

[L1] Something there is that doesn't love a wall,
[L2] That sends the frozen-ground-swell under it,
[L3] And spills the upper boulders in the sun;
[L4] And makes gaps even two can pass abreast.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Personification** (per-sah-nif-ih-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Attributing human qualities to non-human things.
**Evidence:** "doesn't love"
**Effect:** Makes natural force seem conscious, willful.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Concrete Diction** (KON-kreet DIK-shun)
**Lines:** L4
**Definition:** Use of specific, tangible language.
**Evidence:** "gaps"
**Effect:** Makes abstract idea (resistance to walls) physically real.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “gaps” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Inversion** (in-VER-zhun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Reversal of normal word order.
**Evidence:** "Something there is"
**Effect:** Creates formal, archaic tone; emphasizes "Something" by fronting it.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.

### **Polysyndeton** (pol-ee-SIN-duh-ton)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Use of multiple conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "And spills"
**Effect:** Creates accumulation of effects; suggests ongoing, relentless process.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
