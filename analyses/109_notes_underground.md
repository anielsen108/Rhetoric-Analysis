# Passage 109: "Underground Man"

**Fyodor Dostoevsky** · *Notes from Underground* · 1864 | translated from Russian)

**Occasion/Pressure:** This narrative passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A first-person voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through anaphoric self-definition, adversative self-contradiction, doubled negation, and embedded unanswered question, Dostoevsky creates syntax of hyper-consciousness—grammar of the underground man's self-defeating, endlessly reflexive thought.

## PASSAGE

[L1] I am a sick man... I am a spiteful man. I am an unattractive man. I believe my liver is diseased. However, I know nothing at all about my disease, and do not know for certain what ails me.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Paradox** (PAIR-uh-doks)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Self-contradictory statement.
**Evidence:** "However, I know nothing"
**Effect:** Shows self-defeating consciousness.
**Near-miss:** Oxymoron is paradox compressed to a two-word collision ('living death'); antithesis balances opposites without asserting both are true.

### **Correctio** (kor-REK-tee-oh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Self-correction.
**Evidence:** "However"
**Effect:** Enacts the self-undermining consciousness of narrator.
**Near-miss:** Aposiopesis abandons the sentence; correctio revises it in flight and keeps going.
