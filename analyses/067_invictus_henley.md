# Passage 067: "Invictus"

**William Ernest Henley** · *Book of Verses* · 1888 | Selected passage

**Occasion/Pressure:** This lyric passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A first-person voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through metaphoric geography, agnostic thanksgiving, and multiply-negated assertion, Henley creates Victorian stoicism. Form IS defiance—syntax emerging OUT OF darkness to assert unconquerable self.

---

## PASSAGE

[L1] Out of the night that covers me,
[L2] Black as the pit from pole to pole,
[L3] I thank whatever gods may be
[L4] For my unconquerable soul.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "night that covers me, Black as the pit"
**Effect:** Makes abstract suffering concrete and spatial.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

### **Simile** (SIM-ih-lee)
**Lines:** L2
**Definition:** Explicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "Black as the pit from pole to pole"
**Effect:** Emphasizes totality of darkness through geographic scope.
**Near-miss:** Drop the 'like' and it becomes metaphor. An epic simile extends the comparison over many lines with its own internal scene.

### **Litotes** (LIE-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L4
**Definition:** Understatement through negation.
**Evidence:** "unconquerable"
**Effect:** Emphasizes invincibility through negative construction.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Asyndeton** (uh-SIN-duh-ton / əˈsɪndətɒn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** A sequence whose internal joins rely more on punctuation than repeated conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "Out of the night that covers me,"
**Effect:** Comma pressure accelerates the sequence and lets the items arrive as one accumulating perception.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
