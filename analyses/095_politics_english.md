## PASSAGE

[L1] The great enemy of clear language is insincerity. When there is a gap between one's real and one's declared aims, one turns as it were instinctively to long words and exhausted idioms, like a cuttlefish spurting out ink.

**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "enemy of clear language"
**Effect:** Makes abstraction concrete; shows language as defensive mechanism.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

### **Simile** (SIM-ih-lee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Explicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "like a cuttlefish spurting out ink"
**Effect:** Visualizes obfuscation as natural defense; makes obscurity biological.
**Near-miss:** Drop the 'like' and it becomes metaphor. An epic simile extends the comparison over many lines with its own internal scene.

### **Personification** (per-sah-nuh-fuh-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Human qualities to abstractions.
**Evidence:** "enemy"
**Effect:** Makes linguistic corruption active agent.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.
