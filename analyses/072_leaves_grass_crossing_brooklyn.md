**Walt Whitman** · *Leaves of Grass* · 1856 | Selected passage

## PASSAGE

[L1] Flood-tide below me! I see you face to face!
[L2] Clouds of the west—sun there half an hour high—I see you also face to face.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Anaphora** (uh-NAF-or-uh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Repetition at beginning of clauses.
**Evidence:** "I see you face to face"
**Effect:** Creates incantatory rhythm; emphasizes direct encounter.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
