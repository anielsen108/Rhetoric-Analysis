# Passage 068: "Sailing to Byzantium"

**William Butler Yeats** · *The Tower* · 1928 | Selected passage

**Occasion/Pressure:** This lyric passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A third-person or impersonal voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through negation, enjambment, and parenthetical interruption, Yeats creates syntax of alienation. Form performs exclusion—syntax that denies belonging while observing beauty from outside.

---

## PASSAGE

[L1] That is no country for old men. The young
[L2] In one another's arms, birds in the trees
[L3] —Those dying generations—at their song

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Negation** (neh-GAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Statement through denial.
**Evidence:** "That is no country for old men"
**Effect:** Defines through exclusion; creates sense of exile.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “That is no country for old men” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Parenthetical Aside** (pair-en-THET-ih-kul)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Interrupting comment.
**Evidence:** "—Those dying generations—"
**Effect:** Intrudes bitter observation into sensual scene.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “—Those dying generations—” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Juxtaposition** (juk-stuh-puh-ZIH-shun)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Contrasting elements placed together.
**Evidence:** "dying generations"
**Effect:** Shows beauty and decay coexist.
**Near-miss:** Antithesis states its contrast in balanced grammar; juxtaposition just sets the two things down and walks away.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Parataxis** (pair-uh-TAK-sis / ˌpærəˈtæksɪs)
**Lines:** L1–3
**Definition:** Independent units placed beside one another with minimal explicit hierarchy.
**Evidence:** "That is no country for old men. The young In one another's arms, birds in the trees —Those dying generations—at their song"
**Effect:** The adjacent statements make the reader infer their relation, giving the transition pressure without over-explaining it.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
