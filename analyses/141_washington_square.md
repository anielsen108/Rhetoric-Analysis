## PASSAGE

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "repertoire"
**Effect:** Makes spontaneous emotion into performed routine.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

### **Irony** (EYE-ruh-nee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Meaning opposite to surface.
**Evidence:** "It must be added that she had a very slender repertoire of"
**Effect:** Shows how patriarchal control extends to facial expressions.
**Near-miss:** Sarcasm is verbal irony weaponized at a target. Paradox asserts a real contradiction; irony merely means other than it says.

### **Litotes** (LY-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Understatement.
**Evidence:** "very slender repertoire"
**Effect:** Creates restrained, polite tone for describing repression.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.
