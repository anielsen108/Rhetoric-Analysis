# Passage 044: A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man

**James Joyce** · *A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man* · 1916 | Chapter 1

**Occasion/Pressure:** This narrative passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A third-person or impersonal voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through baby-talk vocabulary, paratactic accumulation, and run-on structure, Joyce creates syntax that IS child consciousness. The form doesn't describe developmental language—it PERFORMS it. We don't observe the child from outside; we experience language…

## PASSAGE

[L1] Once upon a time and a very good time it was there was a moocow coming down along the road and this moocow that was coming down along the road met a nicens little boy named baby tuckoo...

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Stream of Consciousness** (streem ov KON-shus-ness)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Narrative presenting unfiltered thought flow.
**Evidence:** "Once upon a time and a very good time it was there"
**Effect:** Places us inside child's consciousness; makes language developmental.
**Near-miss:** Free indirect discourse keeps the narrator's grammatical order while borrowing the character's voice; stream of consciousness lets the order itself dissolve.

### **Fairy Tale Convention** (FAIR-ee tayl kon-VEN-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Use of traditional story opening.
**Evidence:** "Once upon a time"
**Effect:** Signals this is a story, but child's version of story.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “Once upon a time” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Neologism** (nee-AHL-oh-jiz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Invented or baby-talk words.
**Evidence:** "baby tuckoo"
**Effect:** Represents child's developing language; makes us experience linguistic formation.
**Near-miss:** Catachresis misuses an existing word; neologism mints a new one. A portmanteau is a neologism made by fusion (slithy = slimy + lithe).

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Parataxis** (pair-uh-TAK-sis)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Coordination without subordination.
**Evidence:** "Once upon a time and a very good time it was there"
**Effect:** Mimics child's storytelling: additive, without hierarchy.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
