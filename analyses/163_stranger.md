## PASSAGE

[L1] Maman died today. Or yesterday maybe, I don't know. I got a telegram from the home: "Mother deceased. Funeral tomorrow. Faithfully yours." That doesn't mean anything. Maybe it was yesterday.

**Evidence:** "Maybe it was yesterday"
**Effect:** Shows circular, uncertain thought process.
**Near-miss:** Ellipsis omits words the reader can reconstruct; aposiopesis abandons the sentence at a cliff edge.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Parataxis** (pair-uh-TAK-sis / ˌpærəˈtæksɪs)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Independent units placed beside one another with minimal explicit hierarchy.
**Evidence:** "Maman died today. Or yesterday maybe, I don't know."
**Effect:** The adjacent statements make the reader infer their relation, giving the transition pressure without over-explaining it.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
