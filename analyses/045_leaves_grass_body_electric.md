## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Neologism** (nee-AHL-oh-jiz-um)
**Lines:** L4
**Definition:** Invented word.
**Evidence:** "discorrupt"
**Effect:** Creates words for concepts that don't have them; expands English's expressive capacity.
**Near-miss:** Catachresis misuses an existing word; neologism mints a new one. A portmanteau is a neologism made by fusion (slithy = slimy + lithe).

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "body electric"
**Effect:** Fuses physical and spiritual through technological metaphor.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.
