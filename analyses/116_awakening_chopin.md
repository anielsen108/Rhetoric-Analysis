## PASSAGE

[L1] The voice of the sea is seductive; never ceasing, whispering, clamoring, murmuring, inviting the soul to wander for a spell in abysses of solitude; to lose itself in mazes of inward contemplation.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Personification** (per-sah-nuh-fuh-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Human qualities to non-human.
**Evidence:** "voice"
**Effect:** Makes nature an active seducer.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "abysses"
**Effect:** Makes psychological states into dangerous spaces.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Asyndeton** (uh-SIN-duh-tahn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Omission of conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "whispering, clamoring, murmuring"
**Effect:** Accelerates accumulation of sea's varied speech.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
