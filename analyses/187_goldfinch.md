## PASSAGE

[L1] Things would have turned out better if she had lived. As it was, she died when I was a kid; and though everything that's happened to me since then is thoroughly my own fault, still when I lost her I lost sight of any landmark that might have led me someplace happier, to some more populated or congenial life.

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "landmark"
**Effect:** Makes maternal guidance into navigational necessity.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

### **Litotes** (LY-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Understatement through negation.
**Evidence:** "some more populated or congenial life"
**Effect:** Understates desired happiness through vague alternatives.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Anaphora** (uh-NAF-or-uh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Repetition at beginning.
**Evidence:** "when I"
**Effect:** Links temporal clauses of loss.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
