## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Euphemism** (YOO-fuh-miz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mild expression for harsh reality.
**Evidence:** "To be with the Good Lord"
**Effect:** Uses religious language to soften mortality for child.
**Near-miss:** Periphrasis is roundabout wording for any reason; euphemism specifically launders the unpleasant.

### **Litotes** (LY-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Understatement through negation.
**Evidence:** "I don't think you're old"
**Effect:** Shows child's innocent negation of father's age.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Anaphora** (uh-NAF-or-uh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Repetition at beginning.
**Evidence:** "and you said"
**Effect:** Creates rhythm of remembered dialogue.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
