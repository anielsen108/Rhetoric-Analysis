## PASSAGE

[L1] In a village of La Mancha, the name of which I have no desire to call to mind, there lived not long since one of those gentlemen that keep a lance in the lance-rack, an old buckler, a lean hack, and a greyhound for coursing.

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Apophasis** (uh-PAH-fuh-sis)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mentioning by claiming not to mention.
**Evidence:** "the name of which I have no desire to call to mind"
**Effect:** Pretends to forget while emphasizing the forgetting; creates unreliable narrator.
**Near-miss:** Paralipsis and praeteritio are near-synonyms; irony is the wider genus of saying-other-than-meaning.

### **Catalog** (KAT-uh-log)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** List of related items.
**Evidence:** "buckler, a lean hack, and a greyhound"
**Effect:** Accumulates details of gentleman's modest possessions.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “buckler, a lean hack, and a greyhound” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

### **Litotes** (LIE-tuh-teez)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Understatement through negation.
**Evidence:** "not long since"
**Effect:** Creates conversational, oral storytelling tone.
**Near-miss:** Meiosis is plain understatement without the negation mechanism; litotes specifically runs on negatives.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Asyndeton** (uh-SIN-duh-ton / əˈsɪndətɒn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** A sequence whose internal joins rely more on punctuation than repeated conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "In a village of La Mancha, the name of which I have no desire to call to mind, there lived not long since one of those gentlemen that keep a lance in the lance-rack, an old buckler, a lean hack, and a greyhound for coursing."
**Effect:** Comma pressure accelerates the sequence and lets the items arrive as one accumulating perception.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
