# Passage 071: Don Quixote Opening

## Author and Source
**Miguel de Cervantes**, *Don Quixote* (translated by Edith Grossman, 1605), Part I, Chapter 1

## Passage
> In a village of La Mancha, the name of which I have no desire to call to mind, there lived not long since one of those gentlemen that keep a lance in the lance-rack, an old buckler, a lean hack, and a greyhound for coursing.

## Rhetorical Tropes

### **Apophasis** (uh-PAH-fuh-sis)
*Definition:* Mentioning by claiming not to mention.
*Example:* "the name of which I have no desire to call to mind"
*Effect:* Pretends to forget while emphasizing the forgetting; creates unreliable narrator.

### **Catalog** (KAT-uh-log)
*Definition:* List of related items.
*Example:* Lance, buckler, hack, greyhound
*Effect:* Accumulates details of gentleman's modest possessions.

### **Litotes** (LIE-tuh-teez)
*Definition:* Understatement through negation.
*Example:* "not long since" for recent past
*Effect:* Creates conversational, oral storytelling tone.

## Syntax as Style Analysis

### Prepositional Opening
"In a village of La Mancha"—**prepositional phrase** of location begins the novel, grounding story geographically before introducing character.

### Relative Clause Interruption
"the name of which I have no desire to call to mind"—**restrictive relative clause** interrupts to claim forgotten name. Syntax through interruption creates conversational aside.

The **negative construction** "have no desire" emphasizes willful forgetting, not accidental.

### Temporal Phrase
"not long since"—**negative temporal phrase** (archaic for "not long ago"). Syntax through negation and archaic phrasing creates oral tale atmosphere.

### Indefinite Subject
"one of those gentlemen"—**indefinite construction** makes protagonist generic initially, one of a type.

### Relative Clause Description
"that keep a lance in the lance-rack"—**restrictive relative clause** characterizes the type of gentleman through possessions.

### Catalog Structure
"a lance...an old buckler, a lean hack, and a greyhound"—**series of noun phrases** each specifying one possession. Syntax through accumulated indefinite articles (a...an...a...and a) creates inventory effect.

The **modifiers** reveal condition: "old" buckler, "lean" hack—syntax shows these are past-their-prime possessions.

### Stylistic Effect
Through claimed forgetting, temporal vagueness, and detailed inventory of modest possessions, Cervantes creates oral storytelling syntax. Form IS the tale-teller—grammar that pretends casualness while precisely cataloguing, that claims forgetting while remembering every detail.
