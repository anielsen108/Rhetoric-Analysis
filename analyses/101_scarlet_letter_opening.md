## PASSAGE

[L1] A throng of bearded men, in sad-colored garments and gray, steeple-crowned hats, intermixed with women, some wearing hoods, and others bareheaded, was assembled in front of a wooden edifice, the door of which was heavily timbered with oak, and studded with iron spikes.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Synecdoche** (sin-EK-duh-kee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Part represents whole.
**Evidence:** "steeple-crowned hats"
**Effect:** Condenses social identity into physical markers.
**Near-miss:** A subtype of metonymy: synecdoche requires the part/whole relation specifically, not just any association.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Asyndeton** (uh-SIN-duh-tahn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Omission of conjunctions between items.
**Evidence:** "sad-colored garments and gray, steeple-crowned hats"
**Effect:** Creates accumulation of descriptive details; emphasizes Puritan austerity.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.

### **Parallelism** (pair-uh-LEL-iz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Similar grammatical structures.
**Evidence:** "some wearing hoods, and others bareheaded"
**Effect:** Balances description; shows variety within uniformity.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
