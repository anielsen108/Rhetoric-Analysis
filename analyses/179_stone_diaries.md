## PASSAGE

[L1] My mother's name was Mercy Stone Goodwill. She was only thirty years old when she took sick, a boiling hot day, standing there in her back kitchen, making a Malvern pudding for her husband's supper.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Euphemism** (YOO-fuh-miz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Mild expression for harsh reality.
**Evidence:** "took sick"
**Effect:** Uses colloquial understatement for tragedy.
**Near-miss:** Periphrasis is roundabout wording for any reason; euphemism specifically launders the unpleasant.

### **Irony** (EYE-ruh-nee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Contrast between expectation and reality.
**Evidence:** "Mercy"
**Effect:** Makes victim's name darkly appropriate.
**Near-miss:** Sarcasm is verbal irony weaponized at a target. Paradox asserts a real contradiction; irony merely means other than it says.

### **Metonymy** (meh-TAH-nuh-mee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Associated term for thing.
**Evidence:** "back kitchen"
**Effect:** Locates death in space of women's labor.
**Near-miss:** Metaphor maps by resemblance; metonymy by association. Synecdoche is the special case where the substitute is a *part* of the whole.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Parataxis** (pair-uh-TAK-sis / ˌpærəˈtæksɪs)

**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
