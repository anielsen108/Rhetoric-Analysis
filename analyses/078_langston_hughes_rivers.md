# Passage 078: "The Negro Speaks of Rivers"

**Langston Hughes** · *The Crisis* · 1921 | Selected passage

**Occasion/Pressure:** This lyric passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A first-person voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.

## PASSAGE

[L1] I've known rivers:
[L2] I've known rivers ancient as the world and older than the
[L3] flow of human blood in human veins.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Hyperbole** (hy-PER-buh-lee)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Exaggeration.
**Evidence:** "flow of human blood"
**Effect:** Makes rivers primordial, connecting speaker to ancient origins.
**Near-miss:** Its opposite pole is understatement/litotes. When exaggeration collapses into the trivial for comic effect, that's bathos.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Anaphora** (uh-NAF-or-uh)
**Lines:** L1–2
**Definition:** Repetition at beginning of clauses.
**Evidence:** "I've known rivers"
**Effect:** Creates incantatory rhythm; emphasizes deep knowledge.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
