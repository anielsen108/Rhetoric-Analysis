## PASSAGE

[L1] The trumpets sounded, and instantly the arena was cleared for the race. The people shouted in a frenzy of gladness, and the earth and the air seemed to tremble with the transport.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Personification** (per-sah-nuh-fuh-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Human qualities to non-human.
**Evidence:** "the earth and the air seemed to tremble"
**Effect:** Makes natural world participate in human excitement.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Hyperbole** (hy-PER-buh-lee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Exaggeration.
**Evidence:** "frenzy of gladness"
**Effect:** Amplifies crowd's excitement to cosmic scale.
**Near-miss:** Its opposite pole is understatement/litotes. When exaggeration collapses into the trivial for comic effect, that's bathos.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Zeugma** (ZOOG-muh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Single verb governing different objects.
**Evidence:** "tremble"
**Effect:** Unifies disparate elements in shared response.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
