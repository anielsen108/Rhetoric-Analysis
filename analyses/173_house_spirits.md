## PASSAGE

[L1] Barrabás came to us by sea, the child Clara wrote in her delicate calligraphy.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Personification** (per-sah-nuh-fuh-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Human qualities to abstractions.
**Evidence:** "by sea"
**Effect:** Makes pet's arrival mythic, heroic.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Metonymy** (meh-TAH-nuh-mee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Associated term for thing.
**Evidence:** "sea"
**Effect:** Elevates prosaic travel to elemental force.
**Near-miss:** Metaphor maps by resemblance; metonymy by association. Synecdoche is the special case where the substitute is a *part* of the whole.

### **Hendiadys** (hen-DY-uh-dis)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Two descriptions for one thing.
**Evidence:** "delicate calligraphy"
**Effect:** Characterizes writer through age and handwriting.
**Near-miss:** Not a mere pair of things: in hendiadys the two nouns secretly name *one* thing.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Phrasal weights arranged so that the sentence resolves in a measured second movement.
**Evidence:** "Barrabás came to us by sea, the child Clara wrote in her delicate calligraphy."
**Effect:** The balanced movement makes the sentence feel composed rather than merely informational.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
