## PASSAGE

[L1] A Saturday afternoon in November was approaching the time of twilight, and the vast tract of unenclosed wild known as Egdon Heath embrowned itself moment by moment.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Personification** (per-sah-nuh-fuh-KAY-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Human qualities to non-human.
**Evidence:** "embrowned itself"
**Effect:** Makes landscape an active agent of its own transformation.
**Near-miss:** Prosopopoeia goes further and gives the thing a speaking voice. Pathetic fallacy is personification of nature specifically, colored by the observer's emotion.

### **Neologism** (nee-AH-luh-jiz-um)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Invented word.
**Evidence:** "embrowned"
**Effect:** Creates precise verb for gradual darkening.
**Near-miss:** Catachresis misuses an existing word; neologism mints a new one. A portmanteau is a neologism made by fusion (slithy = slimy + lithe).

### **Metonymy** (meh-TAH-nuh-mee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Associated term substituting.
**Evidence:** "November"
**Effect:** Emphasizes temporal specificity.
**Near-miss:** Metaphor maps by resemblance; metonymy by association. Synecdoche is the special case where the substitute is a *part* of the whole.
