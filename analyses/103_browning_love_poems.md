# Passage 103: "How Do I Love Thee?"

**Elizabeth Barrett Browning** · *Sonnets from the Portuguese* · 1850 | Selected passage

**Occasion/Pressure:** This lyric passage must establish its controlling situation while making a small excerpt bear the work's larger pressure.
**Persona/Stance:** A first-person voice controls what the reader can know and how quickly the scene or proposition comes into focus.
**Thesis of Effect:** Through rhetorical question, polysyndetic enumeration, modal capacity, and abstract prepositional objects, Browning creates syntax of measured transcendence—grammar that quantifies the infinite.

---

## PASSAGE

[L1] How do I love thee? Let me count the ways.
[L2] I love thee to the depth and breadth and height
[L3] My soul can reach, when feeling out of sight
[L4] For the ends of being and ideal grace.

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Metaphor** (MET-uh-for)
**Lines:** L2
**Definition:** Implicit comparison.
**Evidence:** "love thee to the depth and breadth and height"
**Effect:** Makes abstract emotion geometrically quantifiable.
**Near-miss:** Simile flags the comparison with like/as, keeping the two things separate. A metaphor sustained across a passage becomes extended metaphor or allegory; a strained, category-breaking one is catachresis.

### **Synesthesia** (sin-es-THEE-zhuh)
**Lines:** L3
**Definition:** Mixing sensory modes.
**Evidence:** "feeling"
**Effect:** Makes spiritual quest physical.
**Near-miss:** The label depends on the relation between “feeling” and the surrounding sentence, not on the phrase in isolation.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Anaphora** (uh-NAF-or-uh)
**Lines:** L1–2
**Definition:** Repetition at beginning.
**Evidence:** "I love thee"
**Effect:** Creates incantatory accumulation of love's measurements.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.

### **Polysyndeton** (pah-lee-SIN-duh-tahn)
**Lines:** L2
**Definition:** Many conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "depth and breadth and height"
**Effect:** Slows pace; makes each dimension significant.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
