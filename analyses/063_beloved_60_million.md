## 2) SYNTAX AS STYLE (Tufte-grade)

**Sentence architecture:** The excerpt uses 1 displayed line and 1 sentence movement. Its grammar distributes attention before, within, and after the main clauses instead of treating word order as a neutral container.

### Numerical Opening
"Sixty Million"—**cardinal number** +  **capitalized common noun**. The capitalization of "Million" gives it weight, importance, treating it almost as proper noun.

The **specific number** (sixty, not "many" or "countless") creates documentary precision while representing approximation—we can't know exact numbers.

### Coordinate Addition
"and more"—the **coordinating conjunction** + **quantifier** suggests the number is minimum, not maximum. The syntax through "and more" makes explicit: this is uncountable.

### Absence of Verb
The dedication contains **no finite verb**—it's a **noun phrase** serving as complete utterance. This creates effect of inscription, like tombstone or memorial. Verbs imply action, time; their absence creates timeless memorial.

### Absence of Preposition
Standard dedication might say "To Sixty Million" or "For Sixty Million." Morrison omits preposition, making the number itself the dedication. The **syntax through omission** creates directness, stark simplicity.

### Capital M
The **capitalized "Million"** treats the number with respect usually reserved for proper nouns or divine names. The syntax through typography elevates.

### Stylistic Effect
Through numerical specificity, syntactic incompletion, and typographical emphasis, Morrison creates memorial that's both precise and infinite. The form IS monument—syntax stripped to essential (number + more), creating space for grief that can't be fully expressed. Grammar as gravestone.
