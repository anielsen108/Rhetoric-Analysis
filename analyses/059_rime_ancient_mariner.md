## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Paradox** (PAIR-uh-doks)
**Lines:** L4
**Definition:** Seemingly contradictory statement containing truth.
**Evidence:** "to drink"
**Effect:** Captures the mariner's curse: nature's plenty turned useless.
**Near-miss:** Oxymoron is paradox compressed to a two-word collision ('living death'); antithesis balances opposites without asserting both are true.

### **Irony** (EYE-ruh-nee)
**Lines:** L1–3
**Definition:** Outcome opposite to what's expected.
**Evidence:** "Water"
**Effect:** Subverts normal associations; makes ocean threatening rather than sustaining.
**Near-miss:** Sarcasm is verbal irony weaponized at a target. Paradox asserts a real contradiction; irony merely means other than it says.
