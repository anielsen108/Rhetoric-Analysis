## PASSAGE

[L1] Phileas Fogg was one of those mathematically exact people, who, never hurried and always ready, are economical of their steps and their motions.

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Oxymoron** (ok-see-MOR-on)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Contradictory terms together.
**Evidence:** "never hurried and always ready"
**Effect:** Shows Fogg's paradoxical combination of calm and preparedness.
**Near-miss:** Paradox is a contradictory *statement* that unfolds; oxymoron is the two-word spark.

### **Metonymy** (meh-TAH-nuh-mee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Associated term for thing.
**Evidence:** "steps and"
**Effect:** Reduces life to mechanical movements.
**Near-miss:** Metaphor maps by resemblance; metonymy by association. Synecdoche is the special case where the substitute is a *part* of the whole.
