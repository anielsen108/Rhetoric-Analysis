## PASSAGE

[L1] One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman. No biological, psychological, or economic fate determines the figure that the human female presents in society; it is civilization as a whole that produces this creature, intermediate between male and eunuch, which is described as feminine.

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Apposition** (ap-uh-ZIH-shun)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Noun phrase restating another.
**Evidence:** "this creature, intermediate between male and eunuch"
**Effect:** Defines woman as absence, negation.
**Near-miss:** Parenthesis interrupts without renaming; apposition always re-identifies the neighboring noun.

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Balanced opposition.
**Evidence:** "not born, but rather becomes"
**Effect:** Separates biology from social construction; emphasizes process over essence.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.

**Definition:** Omission of conjunctions.
