## PASSAGE

[L1] Brrrrrrriiiiiiiiiiing! An alarm clock clanged in the dark and silent room. A bed spring creaked. A woman's voice sang out impatiently: "Bigger, shut that thing off!"

---

## A) RHETORICAL TROPES USED

### **Onomatopoeia** (ah-nuh-mat-uh-PEE-uh)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Words imitating sounds.
**Evidence:** "Brrrrrrriiiiiiiiiiing!"
**Effect:** Jolts reader into Bigger's harsh awakening.
**Near-miss:** Alliteration and assonance pattern sounds without imitating anything; onomatopoeia requires the sound-sense match.

### **Metonymy** (meh-TAH-nuh-mee)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Substituting associated term.
**Evidence:** "A woman's voice"
**Effect:** Depersonalizes, shows Bigger's alienation even from family.
**Near-miss:** Metaphor maps by resemblance; metonymy by association. Synecdoche is the special case where the substitute is a *part* of the whole.

---

## B) RHETORICAL SCHEMES USED

### **Asyndeton** (uh-SIN-duh-tahn)
**Lines:** L1
**Definition:** Omission of conjunctions.
**Evidence:** "Brrrrrrriiiiiiiiiiing"
**Effect:** Creates staccato rhythm; mimics abrupt awakening.
**Risk:** Repeated mechanically, this pattern could become mannered; here its local placement keeps it functional.
