Stylistic Effect

Through present-tense memory, metaphoric seesaw apposition, dense participial qualification, reflexive discovery, and oscillating compound verbs, James creates syntax of psychological ambivalence—grammar that performs narrator's instability through syntactic hesitation and self-correction.

Information flow: The opening establishes “I remember the whole beginning as a succession of flights and drops, a little seesaw of…”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “After rising, in town, to meet his appeal, I had at all events a couple of very bad….”

Micro-rewrites

Compression test: Reduce the excerpt to one independent clause. The informational core remains, but the pacing, qualification, and hierarchy identified above disappear.

Expansion test: Convert its modifiers into separate sentences. Clarity may increase locally, but the original relations among perceptions, actions, and judgments become less simultaneous.
