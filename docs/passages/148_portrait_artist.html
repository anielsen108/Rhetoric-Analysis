Stylistic Effect

Through fairy tale formula, existential construction, progressive participles, redundant relative clause, polysyndetic coordination, and neologistic phonetics, Joyce creates syntax of infantile consciousness—grammar that renders narrative through the linguistic structures of early childhood, collapsing distance between teller and told.

Information flow: The opening establishes “Once upon a time and a very good time it was there was a moocow coming down along the…”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “Once upon a time and a very good time it was there was a moocow coming down along the….”

Faultlines

  1. Overstatement risk: Making the neologism more explicit would explain the phrase at the cost of the inference the reader currently performs.
  2. Pattern risk: Extending anaphora beyond its present span could turn designed emphasis into mannerism.
  3. Paraphrase risk: Replacing “baby tuckoo” with its abstract meaning would preserve information but remove voice, rhythm, and the passage's interpretive invitation.
