Perfect Tense Opening

"I have...studied"—present perfect tense connects past action (studying) to present state (still affected by it). Syntax makes past study relevant to current despair.

Interjection

"alas"—parenthetical interjection interrupts syntax to express emotion. Placed after auxiliary but before participle, it colors the entire predicate with regret.
