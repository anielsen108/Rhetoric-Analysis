Stylistic Effect

Through paradoxical construction (beauty by plainness), passive throwing into relief, comparative result linking form to possibility, and religious-artistic allusion, Eliot creates syntax of idealizing narration—grammar that elevates prosaic subject through high cultural reference.

Information flow: The opening establishes “Miss Brooke had that kind of beauty which seems to be thrown into relief by poor dress.”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “Her hand and wrist were so finely formed that she could wear sleeves not less bare of….”

3Deixis, Aspect, Modality

Deictic center: The main orienting terms are “that”, “she”, “those”. They locate the implied speaker, audience, scene, or temporal vantage from which the passage becomes intelligible.

Aspect and tense: Past-tense signals (“had”, “were”) place the represented action behind the speaking moment.

Modality: The excerpt uses “could”. This matters because modality marks whether a proposition is asserted as fact, entertained as possibility, imposed as duty, or projected as intention.
