Stylistic Effect

Through negation, enjambment, and parenthetical interruption, Yeats creates syntax of alienation. Form performs exclusion—syntax that denies belonging while observing beauty from outside.

Information flow: The opening establishes “That is no country for old men.”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “The young In one another's arms, birds in the trees —Those dying generations—at their song.”
