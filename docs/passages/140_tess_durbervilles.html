Stylistic Effect

Through ironic scare quotes, passive completion, classical allusion as authorization, past perfect finality, and genitive making Tess object of divine sport, Hardy creates syntax of cosmic injustice—grammar that indicts both human law ("Justice") and divine order (President's "sport") through bitter irony and classical elevation.

Information flow: The opening establishes “"Justice" was done, and the President of the Immortals, in Aeschylean phrase, had ended…”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “"Justice" was done, and the President of the Immortals, in Aeschylean phrase, had ended….”
