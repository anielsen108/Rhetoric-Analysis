Stylistic Effect

Through numerical precision, spatial preposition placing living on dead, interjective refrain, coordinate subjects linking drink and devil, and past perfect completion, Stevenson creates syntax of pirate ballad—grammar that combines gruesome content with jaunty rhythm, making death casual through musical structure.

Information flow: The opening establishes “Fifteen men on the dead man's chest—Yo-ho-ho, and a bottle of rum!”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “Drink and the devil had done for the rest—Yo-ho-ho, and a bottle of rum!.”
