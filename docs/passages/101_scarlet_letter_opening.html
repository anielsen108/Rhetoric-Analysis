8Hotspots & Faultlines

Hotspots

  1. “steeple-crowned hats” — Synecdoche. Condenses social identity into physical markers.
  2. “sad-colored garments and gray, steeple-crowned hats” — Asyndeton. Creates accumulation of descriptive details; emphasizes Puritan austerity.
  3. “some wearing hoods, and others bareheaded” — Parallelism. Balances description; shows variety within uniformity.

Faultlines

  1. Overstatement risk: Making the synecdoche more explicit would explain the phrase at the cost of the inference the reader currently performs.
  2. Pattern risk: Extending asyndeton beyond its present span could turn designed emphasis into mannerism.
  3. Paraphrase risk: Replacing “steeple-crowned hats” with its abstract meaning would preserve information but remove voice, rhythm, and the passage's interpretive invitation.
