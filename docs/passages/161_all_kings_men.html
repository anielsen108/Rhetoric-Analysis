Adversative Opening

"But now"—coordinating conjunction with temporal adverb. Syntax signals reversal from prior belief.

Restrictive Adverb

"that there is no one else"—subordinate clause with existential negative. Syntax makes absence the known fact.

Stylistic Effect

Through adversative now-reversal, epistemic negation, restrictive only-knowing, existential no-one-else, vague what-has-to-be-done infinitive, and tautological I-am-the-one-who-has-to-do-it, Warren creates syntax of reluctant necessity—grammar showing how narrator moves from uncertainty to burden through logical elimination, where duty emerges not from knowledge but from absence of alternatives.

Information flow: The opening establishes “But now I don't know.”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “I only know that there is no one else to do what has to be done, and that I am the one….”

8Hotspots & Faultlines

Hotspots

  1. “I am the one” — Tautology. Emphasizes isolation through logical redundancy.
  2. “and that I am” — Anaphora. Creates parallel certainties after uncertainty.
  3. “now I don't know” — Antithesis. Contrasts epistemological uncertainty with practical certainty.

Faultlines

  1. Overstatement risk: Making the tautology more explicit would explain the phrase at the cost of the inference the reader currently performs.
  2. Pattern risk: Extending anaphora beyond its present span could turn designed emphasis into mannerism.
  3. Paraphrase risk: Replacing “I am the one” with its abstract meaning would preserve information but remove voice, rhythm, and the passage's interpretive invitation.
