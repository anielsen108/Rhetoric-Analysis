Stylistic Effect

Through numerical subject 124, copulative spiteful character, fragmentary full-of elaboration, oxymoronic baby's-venom genitive, definite women-in-house, epistemic knowing, and coordinate children-too, Morrison creates syntax of haunting—grammar that makes house address into malevolent character, breaks standard sentence structure, combines infant innocence with poison, establishing supernatural presence known by all inhabitants.

Information flow: The opening establishes “124 was spiteful.”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “The women in the house knew it and so did the children..”

8Hotspots & Faultlines

Hotspots

  1. “was spiteful” — Personification. Makes address into malevolent entity.
  2. “baby's venom” — Oxymoron. Combines innocence with poison.
  3. “124” — Metonymy. Reduces haunted house to street number.

Faultlines

  1. Overstatement risk: Making the personification more explicit would explain the phrase at the cost of the inference the reader currently performs.
  2. Pattern risk: Extending oxymoron beyond its present span could turn designed emphasis into mannerism.
  3. Paraphrase risk: Replacing “was spiteful” with its abstract meaning would preserve information but remove voice, rhythm, and the passage's interpretive invitation.
