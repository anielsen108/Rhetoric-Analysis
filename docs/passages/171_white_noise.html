Stylistic Effect

Through passive rearrangement removing agency, vague one-day timing, negative without-warning, existential there-is construction, escalating agitation-and-panic coordination, spatial in-aisles/in-faces mapping, and genitive older-shoppers specification, DeLillo creates syntax of postmodern dislocation—grammar that treats consumer disruption with language of catastrophe, satirizing how loss of familiar brands generates existential crisis.

Information flow: The opening establishes “The supermarket shelves have been rearranged.”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “There is agitation and panic in the aisles, dismay in the faces of older shoppers..”
