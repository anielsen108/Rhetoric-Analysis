Syntax

Opening Statement

"Two roads diverged in a yellow wood"—simple subject-verb-prepositional phrase. The past tense immediately places us in retrospect. The syntax is deceptively simple for what becomes a complex meditation.

Apology and Impossibility

"And sorry I could not travel both"—the syntax omits "I was/am" before "sorry," creating compression. The modal negative "could not" expresses impossibility, not unwillingness.

The Constraint

"And be one traveler"—this phrase explains WHY he couldn't travel both. The syntax makes the constraint existential: being ONE traveler means choosing ONE path. The number creates the dilemma.

Duration

"long I stood"—inverted syntax (normally "I stood long") creates archaic, formal tone. The adverb "long" emphasizes the duration of deliberation.

Visual Limitation

"as far as I could / To where it bent"—the syntax emphasizes the limit of vision. We can only see SO FAR, then the path curves away. This visual limitation becomes metaphor for inability to foresee consequences of choices.
