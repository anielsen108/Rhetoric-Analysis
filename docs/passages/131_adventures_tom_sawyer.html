Stylistic Effect

Through personified emotional departure/arrival, fragment of measurement, copulative existential despair, and bathos of philosophical language for fence-painting, Twain creates syntax of comic proportion—grammar that performs boyhood melodrama through ironic elevation of trivial to cosmic.

Information flow: The opening establishes “Tom appeared on the sidewalk with a bucket of whitewash and a long-handled brush.”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “Life to him seemed hollow, and existence but a burden..”
