Stylistic Effect

Through copulative assertion of seduction, asyndetic participial accumulation, purposive infinitives of wandering and loss, and spatial metaphors of abysses and mazes, Chopin creates syntax of dangerous interiority—grammar that makes contemplation and solitude as threatening as they are alluring.

Information flow: The opening establishes “The voice of the sea is seductive; never ceasing, whispering, clamoring, murmuring,…”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “The voice of the sea is seductive; never ceasing, whispering, clamoring, murmuring,….”
