Stylistic Effect

Through layered spatial prepositions, odd adjectival flower-spaces, modal perception, unclear pronouns, objectless hitting, progressive movement toward incomprehensible flag, and paratactic coordinate response, Faulkner creates syntax of cognitive disability—grammar that performs Benjy's consciousness through fragmented deixis and inability to subordinate, name, or causally connect events.

3Deixis, Aspect, Modality

Deictic center: The main orienting terms are “i”, “they”. They locate the implied speaker, audience, scene, or temporal vantage from which the passage becomes intelligible.

Aspect and tense: Past-tense signals (“were”, “was”, “went”) place the represented action behind the speaking moment.

Modality: The excerpt uses “could”. This matters because modality marks whether a proposition is asserted as fact, entertained as possibility, imposed as duty, or projected as intention.
