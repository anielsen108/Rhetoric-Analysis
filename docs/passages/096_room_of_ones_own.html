3Deixis, Aspect, Modality

Deictic center: The main orienting terms are “she”, “that”, “you”. They locate the implied speaker, audience, scene, or temporal vantage from which the passage becomes intelligible.

Aspect and tense: The excerpt relies more on lexical verbs and states than on an explicitly marked retrospective frame.

Modality: The excerpt uses “must”, “will”. This matters because modality marks whether a proposition is asserted as fact, entertained as possibility, imposed as duty, or projected as intention.

Authority effect: The grammar of person, time, and possibility quietly establishes who may name reality and how firmly the reader is asked to accept that naming.

8Hotspots & Faultlines

Hotspots

  1. “unsolved” — Litotes. Dryly acknowledges what material conditions can't resolve.
  2. “as you will see” — Parenthesis. Creates conversational intimacy with reader.
  3. “the true nature of woman and the true nature of fiction” — Parallelism. Links woman and fiction as equally mysterious, equally constructed.

Faultlines

  1. Overstatement risk: Making the litotes more explicit would explain the phrase at the cost of the inference the reader currently performs.
  2. Pattern risk: Extending parenthesis beyond its present span could turn designed emphasis into mannerism.
  3. Paraphrase risk: Replacing “unsolved” with its abstract meaning would preserve information but remove voice, rhythm, and the passage's interpretive invitation.
