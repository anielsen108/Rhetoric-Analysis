Stylistic Effect

Through modal passive obligation, slender repertoire metaphor, passive production removing agency, and prepositional cause making father the recommender of daughter's facial expressions, James creates syntax of patriarchal micromanagement—grammar showing how Dr. Sloper controls even Catherine's spontaneous emotional displays.

Information flow: The opening establishes “It must be added that she had a very slender repertoire of smiles, and that these were…”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “It must be added that she had a very slender repertoire of smiles, and that these were….”

3Deixis, Aspect, Modality

Deictic center: The main orienting terms are “it”, “that”, “she”, “these”. They locate the implied speaker, audience, scene, or temporal vantage from which the passage becomes intelligible.

Aspect and tense: Past-tense signals (“had”, “were”) place the represented action behind the speaking moment.

Modality: The excerpt uses “must”. This matters because modality marks whether a proposition is asserted as fact, entertained as possibility, imposed as duty, or projected as intention.

Authority effect: The grammar of person, time, and possibility quietly establishes who may name reality and how firmly the reader is asked to accept that naming.
