Stylistic Effect

Through definite article suggesting known referent, exclamatory fragments lacking verbs, immediate repetition without variation, and absolute refusal of elaboration, Conrad creates syntax of unspeakable revelation—grammar that breaks down at the limit of what language can articulate, leaving only repeated naming of horror itself.

Information flow: The opening establishes “The horror!”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “The horror!.”
