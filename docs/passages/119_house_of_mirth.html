Syntax

Passive Construction

"the civilization which had produced her"—passive perfect with relative clause. Syntax emphasizes Lily as product, not agent.

Adverbial Emphasis

"so evidently"—intensifying adverb. Syntax makes victimhood obvious, undeniable.

Relative Clause Specification

"which had produced her"—past perfect relative clause. Syntax shows civilization's prior creation of the victim it now destroys.

Result Clause

"that the links...seemed like manacles"—result clause following "so evidently." Syntax makes jewelry's imprisonment logical consequence of visible victimhood.

Participial Bondage

"chaining her to her fate"—present participle with prepositional object. Syntax performs the binding through continuing action.

Possessive Fate

"her fate"—possessive construction. Syntax makes destiny both personal and inescapable.

Stylistic Effect

Through passive production, evidential adverb, resultative syntax, and participial chaining, Wharton creates grammar of determinism—syntax that shows how Gilded Age women are manufactured by civilization then imprisoned by its ornaments, their jewels becoming manacles in a system that offers no escape.

Information flow: The opening establishes “She was so evidently the victim of the civilization which had produced her, that the…”; subsequent material converts that initial footing into the new emphasis carried by “She was so evidently the victim of the civilization which had produced her, that the….”
